How it works

Three conversations. That's it.

No consultation waitlists, no design committee. You talk to Ron, he looks at your yard, and you get a clear answer.

1

You call or email Ron directly

Tell him what you're thinking. Could be specific—"I need a patio"—or vague—"my backyard is a mess." Either works. No pressure, no sales pitch. He'll ask a few questions and set up a time to see the property.

2

Ron visits and gives you a fixed quote

He walks the yard, talks through options, and sends you a written quote within a few days. Materials, labor, timeline—all spelled out. If it doesn't fit your budget, he'll tell you what can be adjusted and what can't.

3

Work starts and finishes on schedule

Ron shows up on the date he committed to. He keeps you informed during the build. When the project wraps, you walk the finished work together. You pay what was quoted. Done.

About Ron

Ten years of showing up in Parker

Ron started MountainCraft in 2015 with a truck, a plan, and a straightforward idea: do residential landscaping for people in Parker, do it well, and don't overcomplicate it.

Ten years later, the operation is the same size by choice. He's not scaling. He's not franchising. He designs every project himself, manages the schedule, and is on-site during the build. When you call MountainCraft, you get Ron.

That's not a limitation—it's the whole point. You're not getting routed to a call center or matched with a random crew. You're hiring a person who's been doing this work in this town for a decade. He knows Parker soil, Parker weather, and Parker HOAs.

Ron's approach is simple: give an honest quote, stick to the timeline, finish the job, and move on to the next one. The repeat customers and referrals take care of the rest.
